The Crater's Legacy

The Ngorongoro Crater formed when a giant volcano exploded and collapsed on itself two to three million years ago. Once higher than Kilimanjaro, the volcano's collapse created the world's largest intact and unflooded volcanic caldera.

Geographic Marvel

Spanning 100 square miles (260 km¬≤) with walls rising up to 2,000 feet (610 meters), the crater creates a natural enclosure for wildlife. The crater floor consists of several distinct areas, including the Lerai Forest, Lake Magadi, and the Ngoitokitok Springs.

Conservation History

Designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1979, the crater is part of the larger Ngorongoro Conservation Area. This unique area allows the coexistence of human settlement and wildlife protection, preserving both natural and cultural heritage.
